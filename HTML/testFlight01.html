Starship TestFlight - 01

    Objective: SN8 aimed to demonstrate the aerodynamic control of the Starship spacecraft during ascent and descent, as well as test the performance of the newly developed Raptor engines.

    Date and Location: The test flight took place on December 9, 2020, from SpaceX's South Texas launch site.
    
    Achievements:
    
    Ascent:  SN8 successfully ascended to an altitude of approximately 12.5 kilometers (41,000 feet).
    Maneuvers: Demonstrated controlled aerodynamic maneuvers during ascent.
    Descent: Initiated a controlled descent phase to test aerodynamic control.
    Raptor Engines: Tested the performance of the newly developed Raptor engines.
    
    Outcome:
    
    Partial Success: While the test achieved several key objectives, one of the Raptor engines failed to reignite properly during the landing phase.
    Hard Landing: This resulted in a hard landing, causing SN8 to crash into the landing pad at high speed and explode upon impact.
    Data Collection: Despite the explosive landing, the test flight provided valuable data for SpaceX engineers, helping to inform future iterations of the Starship design.
    
    Significance: The test demonstrated significant progress towards SpaceX's goal of developing a fully reusable spacecraft capable of carrying humans and cargo to destinations such as the Moon, Mars, and beyond.
